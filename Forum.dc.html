Reducing black-grass in winter wheat

PB
Pieter Bakker
Arable farmer, Groningen · Posted 12 March 2026
{{ tagsEl }}

Black-grass pressure has crept up over three seasons on our heavier fields, despite stale seedbeds and delayed drilling. We are now seeing patches that clearly knock yield, and I want to get ahead of it before it becomes unmanageable.

My main question: for those of you on similar clay soils in the north, what combination of cultural controls actually held the population back — and over how many seasons did you see a real difference?

What I have tried

  • Delayed drilling to mid-October, which helped but cost us establishment in the wet autumn.
  • A spring crop in the rotation every third year.
  • Higher seed rates to improve crop competition.
